### [1] [needLR: Long-read structural variant annotation with population-scale frequency estimation](https://arxiv.org/abs/2512.08175)
*Jonas A. Gustafson,Jiadong Lin,Evan E. Eichler,Danny E. Miller*

Main category: q-bio.GN

TL;DR: needLR是一个用于长读长测序数据的结构变异注释工具，通过群体等位基因频率、基因组上下文注释和基因-表型关联来筛选和优先排序候选致病性结构变异。


<details>
  <summary>Details</summary>
Motivation: 从长读长测序数据中识别致病性结构变异面临挑战，需要有效的过滤和优先排序工具来减少假阳性结果，同时保留真正的致病变异。

Method: needLR使用bash实现，依赖Truvari、BEDTools和BCFtools等工具，整合群体等位基因频率数据、基因组上下文注释和基因-表型关联信息来注释结构变异。

Result: 在9个已知致病性结构变异的测试案例中，needLR为超过97.5%的检测到的SV分配了等位基因频率，将每个病例的平均新型基因相关SV减少到121个，同时保留了所有已知致病变异。

Conclusion: needLR是一个有效的结构变异注释工具，能够显著减少需要进一步分析的候选变异数量，同时保持对已知致病变异的敏感性，有助于长读长测序数据中致病性结构变异的识别。

Abstract: Summary: We present needLR, a structural variant (SV) annotation tool that can be used for filtering and prioritization of candidate pathogenic SVs from long-read sequencing data using population allele frequencies, annotations for genomic context, and gene-phenotype associations. When using population data from 500 presumably healthy individuals to evaluate nine test cases with known pathogenic SVs, needLR assigned allele frequencies to over 97.5% of all detected SVs and reduced the average number of novel genic SVs to 121 per case while retaining all known pathogenic variants. Availability and Implementation: needLR is implemented in bash with dependencies including Truvari v4.2.2, BEDTools v2.31.1, and BCFtools v1.19. Source code, documentation, and pre-computed population allele frequency data are freely available at https://github.com/jgust1/needLR under an MIT license.

Although demonstrated here for vaccine development, this workflow can be adapted for diverse neoantigen therapies and experiments. Therefore, this protocol provides the research community with a reproducible, version-controlled framework for designing personalized neoantigen vaccines, supported by detailed documentation, example datasets, and open-source code.

Main category: q-bio.QM

<details>
  <summary>Details</summary>
Motivation: 肺癌是全球癌症相关死亡的主要原因，需要通过低剂量CT进行精确早期检测。深度学习在肺结节检测和分类方面有改进，但受限于标注数据不足和不同影像中心间的变异性。

Method: 提出CT驱动的半监督框架，采用Inf-Net架构，结合多尺度特征聚合、反向注意力机制和伪标签技术，有效利用未标注的CT切片。

Result: 在LUNA16数据集子集上，监督Inf-Net在10,000标注切片上得分为0.825；半监督变体在20,000切片（包含标注和伪标注数据）上得分为0.784，超过其监督基线0.755。

This research presents a CT-driven, semi-supervised framework utilizing the Inf-Net architecture to enhance lung nodule analysis with minimal annotation. The model incorporates multi-scale feature aggregation, Reverse Attention refinement, and pseudo-labeling to efficiently utilize unlabeled CT slices. Experiments conducted on subsets of the LUNA16 dataset indicate that the supervised Inf-Net attains a score of 0.825 on 10,000 labeled slices. In contrast, the semi-supervised variant achieves a score of 0.784 on 20,000 slices that include both labeled and pseudo-labeled data, thus surpassing its supervised baseline of 0.755. This study presents a conceptual framework for the integration of genomic biomarkers with CT-derived features, facilitating the development of future multimodal, biologically informed CAD systems. The proposed semi-supervised Inf-Net framework improves CT-based lung nodule assessment and lays the groundwork for flexible multi-omics diagnostic models.

<details>
  <summary>Details</summary>

Result: 模型显示温度不仅影响基本反应速度，还通过调控、反馈或随机转换等相互作用影响系统行为。不同建模策略将分子过程与生理温度响应曲线联系起来。

Abstract: Virtually every biological rate changes with temperature, but the mechanisms underlying these responses differ between different processes. These models highlight how temperature influences not only the speed of elementary reactions, but also the behavior that emerges when many reactions interact through regulation, feedback, or stochastic transitions. By outlining the assumptions and implications of each perspective, we aim to clarify how different modeling strategies connect molecular processes to physiological temperature response curves and to point toward integrative frameworks that can better explain the diversity of biological thermal responses.
