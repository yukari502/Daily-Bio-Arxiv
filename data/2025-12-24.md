### [1] [SynCraft: Guiding Large Language Models to Predict Edit Sequences for Molecular Synthesizability Optimization](https://arxiv.org/abs/2512.20333)
*Junren Li,Luhua Lai*

Main category: cs.AI

TL;DR: SynCraft是一个基于推理的框架，将合成可行性优化重构为精确的结构编辑问题，利用大语言模型的推理能力，通过原子级编辑序列预测而非直接生成SMILES字符串，显著提升生成分子的合成可行性。


<details>
  <summary>Details</summary>
Motivation: 生成式AI在化学空间探索中取得革命性进展，但大量生成分子合成不可行。现有方法（如后过滤或基于模板的投影）往往损害结构新颖性或破坏关键药效团。需要一种能保持结构新颖性同时提升合成可行性的方法。

Method: 将合成可行性优化重构为结构编辑问题而非序列翻译任务。利用大语言模型的推理能力，预测可执行的原子级编辑序列，而非直接生成SMILES字符串。通过交互感知提示，复制药物化学专家的直觉。

Result: 在广泛基准测试中，SynCraft在生成具有高结构保真度的可合成类似物方面优于最先进的基线方法。成功复制了药物化学专家对PLK1抑制剂的编辑直觉，并拯救了先前分子生成文献中评分高但被丢弃的RIPK1候选分子。

Conclusion: SynCraft通过将合成可行性优化重构为结构编辑问题，利用大语言模型的推理能力，有效解决了"合成悬崖"问题，在保持结构新颖性和药效团完整性的同时显著提升生成分子的合成可行性。

Abstract: Generative artificial intelligence has revolutionized the exploration of chemical space, yet a critical bottleneck remains that a substantial fraction of generated molecules is synthetically inaccessible. Current solutions, such as post-hoc filtering or projection-based methods, often compromise structural novelty or disrupt key pharmacophores by forcing molecules into pre-defined synthetic templates. Herein, we introduce SynCraft, a reasoning-based framework that reframes synthesizability optimization not as a sequence translation task, but as a precise structural editing problem. Leveraging the emergent reasoning capabilities of Large Language Models, SynCraft navigates the "synthesis cliff" where minimal structural modifications yield significant gains in synthetic feasibility. By predicting executable sequences of atom-level edits rather than generating SMILES strings directly, SynCraft circumvents the syntactic fragility of LLMs while harnessing their chemical intuition. Extensive benchmarks demonstrate that SynCraft outperforms state-of-the-art baselines in generating synthesizable analogs with high structural fidelity. Furthermore, through interaction-aware prompting, SynCraft successfully replicates expert medicinal chemistry intuition in editing PLK1 inhibitors and rescuing high-scoring but previously discarded RIPK1 candidates in previous molecular generation literatures.

### [2] [Methods for Analyzing RNA Pseudoknots via Chord Diagrams and Intersection Graphs](https://arxiv.org/abs/2512.19939)
*Rayan Ibrahim,Allison H. Moore*

Main category: q-bio.BM

TL;DR: 提出基于图论的RNA假结枚举与分类方法，引入距离度量τ分析弦图交点图，通过加权顶点覆盖形式化定义假结，算法能枚举假结并分类二级结构，对三维拓扑特征敏感。


<details>
  <summary>Details</summary>
Motivation: RNA分子形成包括假结在内的复杂二级结构，需要系统框架来枚举、分类和预测这些结构以确定其生物学意义。虽然弦图广泛用于表示RNA二级结构，但假结的数学严格枚举仍然是一个挑战。

Method: 引入基于距离的度量τ来分析与假结结构相关的弦图的交点图。形式化定义假结为特定交点图的加权顶点覆盖，该图从表示RNA核苷酸序列的弦图分区构建。提出严格的图论算法来枚举假结、分类二级结构，并对三维拓扑特征敏感。

Result: 在MATLAB中实现方法，并在bpRNA-1m数据库的假结结构上进行测试。结果证实属（genus）是假结复杂性的稳健量化指标。

Conclusion: 提出的图论方法为RNA假结提供了数学严格的枚举和分类框架，通过交点图和加权顶点覆盖形式化定义假结，算法能有效分析结构复杂性，属被证明是量化假结复杂性的可靠度量。

Abstract: RNA molecules are known to form complex secondary structures including pseudoknots. A systematic framework for the enumeration, classification and prediction of secondary structures is critical to determine the biological significance of the molecular configurations of RNA. Chord diagrams are mathematical objects widely used to represent RNA secondary structures and to analyze structural motifs, however a mathematically rigorous enumeration of pseudoknots remains a challenge. We introduce a method that incorporates a distance-based metric $τ$ to analyze the intersection graph of a chord diagram associated with a pseudoknotted structure. In particular, our method formally defines a pseudoknot in terms of a weighted vertex cover of a certain intersection graph constructed from a partition of the chord diagram representing the nucleotide sequence of the RNA molecule. In this graph-theoretic context, we introduce a rigorous algorithm that enumerates pseudoknots, classifies secondary structures, and is sensitive to three-dimensional topological features. We implement our methods in MATLAB and test the algorithm on pseudoknotted structures from the bpRNA-1m database. Our findings confirm that genus is a robust quantifier of pseudoknot complexity.
