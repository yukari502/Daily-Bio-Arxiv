Abstract: Measurements of cell size dynamics have established the adder principle as a robust mechanism of cell size homeostasis. Here, we extend this framework by considering a general growth law modeled as a Hill-type function of cell size. Importantly, despite these changes, the adder property is preserved. Our results provide a generalized framework for connecting multi-step adder mechanisms with proliferation dynamics, extending size control theory beyond exponential growth.
