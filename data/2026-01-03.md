Here, we extend this framework by considering a general growth law modeled as a Hill-type function of cell size. This assumption introduces growth saturation to the model, such that very large cells grow approximately linearly rather than exponentially. Finally, we analyze stochastic clonal proliferation and find that growth saturation influences both single-cell size statistics and variability across populations. Our results provide a generalized framework for connecting multi-step adder mechanisms with proliferation dynamics, extending size control theory beyond exponential growth.
