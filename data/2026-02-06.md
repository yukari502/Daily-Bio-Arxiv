### [1] [AFD-INSTRUCTION: A Comprehensive Antibody Instruction Dataset with Functional Annotations for LLM-Based Understanding and Design](https://arxiv.org/abs/2602.04916)
*Ling Luo,Wenbin Jiang,Xushi Zhang,Hongyuan Chang,Xinkang Wang,Yueting Xiong,Mengsha Tong,Rongshan Yu*

Main category: q-bio.QM

TL;DR: AFD-Instruction是首个针对抗体的大规模指令数据集，包含抗体理解和抗体设计两个组件，通过自然语言指令连接抗体序列与功能描述，提升LLMs在抗体相关任务上的表现。


<details>
  <summary>Details</summary>
Motivation: 尽管大语言模型在蛋白质表示学习方面取得显著进展，但其通过自然语言解释和设计抗体的能力仍然有限，需要专门的指令数据集来连接抗体序列与功能描述。

Method: 构建AFD-Instruction数据集，包含两个关键组件：1) 抗体理解：从序列推断功能属性；2) 抗体设计：在功能约束下进行从头序列生成。该数据集提供明确的序列-功能对齐，支持基于自然语言指令的抗体设计。

Result: 在通用大语言模型上进行广泛的指令调优实验表明，AFD-Instruction能持续提升多种抗体相关任务的性能，包括功能属性推断和受约束的序列生成。

Conclusion: AFD-Instruction通过连接抗体序列与功能文本描述，为推进抗体建模和加速治疗发现建立了新的基础，填补了LLMs在抗体解释和设计方面的能力空白。

Abstract: Large language models (LLMs) have significantly advanced protein representation learning. However, their capacity to interpret and design antibodies through natural language remains limited. To address this challenge, we present AFD-Instruction, the first large-scale instruction dataset with functional annotations tailored to antibodies. This dataset encompasses two key components: antibody understanding, which infers functional attributes directly from sequences, and antibody design, which enables de novo sequence generation under functional constraints. These components provide explicit sequence-function alignment and support antibody design guided by natural language instructions. Extensive instruction-tuning experiments on general-purpose LLMs demonstrate that AFD-Instruction consistently improves performance across diverse antibody-related tasks. By linking antibody sequences with textual descriptions of function, AFD-Instruction establishes a new foundation for advancing antibody modeling and accelerating therapeutic discovery.

### [2] [Learning virulence-transmission relationships using causal inference](https://arxiv.org/abs/2602.05196)
*Sudam Surasinghe,C. Brandon Ogbunugafor*

Main category: q-bio.PE

TL;DR: LETR框架通过整合因果发现与生成映射，揭示病原体毒力与传播之间的动态关系，挑战了传统静态模型假设。


<details>
  <summary>Details</summary>
Motivation: 传统模型通常假设病原体特征之间存在静态、固定的联系，但识别性状之间的方向性和机制性关系仍然是生物学各子领域的关键挑战。需要数据驱动的方法来理解毒力与传播等性状如何随时间动态变化。

Method: 提出学习进化性状关系（LETR）框架，应用格兰杰因果原理确定哪些性状驱动其他性状以及这些关系如何随时间变化。该框架整合了因果发现、生成映射和转移算子分析，将短期可预测性与长期性状分布联系起来。

Result: 在合成粘液瘤病毒-宿主数据集中，LETR可靠地恢复了已知的方向性影响（如毒力驱动传播）。应用于全球SARS-CoV-2大流行数据时，发现过去的毒力改善了未来传播预测，而反向效应较弱。不变密度估计揭示了向低毒力和低传播的长期趋势，毒力的双峰性表明生态影响或宿主异质性。

Conclusion: 病原体毒力与传播之间的关系高度特异且依赖于情境，这削弱了简单化模型的有效性，并鼓励发展新的病原体进化约束理论。LETR框架通过统一因果推断与动力学建模，为揭示各种复杂生物系统中的机制性性状联系提供了通用方法。

Abstract: The relationship between traits that influence pathogen virulence and transmission is part of the central canon of the evolution and ecology of infectious disease. However, identifying directional and mechanistic relationships among traits remains a key challenge in various subfields of biology, as models often assume static, fixed links between characteristics. Here, we introduce learning evolutionary trait relationships (LETR), a data-driven framework that applies Granger-causality principles to determine which traits drive others and how these relationships change over time. LETR integrates causal discovery with generative mapping and transfer-operator analysis to link short-term predictability with long-term trait distributions. Using a synthetic myxomatosis virus-host data set, we show that LETR reliably recovers known directional influences, such as virulence driving transmission. Applying the framework to global pandemic (SARS-CoV-2) data, we find that past virulence improves future transmission prediction, while the reverse effect is weak. Invariant-density estimates reveal a long-term trend toward low virulence and transmission, with bimodality in virulence suggesting ecological influences or host heterogeneity. In summary, this study provides a blueprint for learning the relationship between how harmful a pathogen is and how well it spreads, which is highly idiosyncratic and context-dependent. This finding undermines simplistic models and encourages the development of new theory for the constraints underlying pathogen evolution. Further, by uniting causal inference with dynamical modeling, the LETR framework offers a general approach for uncovering mechanistic trait linkages in complex biological systems of various kinds.
