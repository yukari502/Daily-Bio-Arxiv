### [1] [ChemRecon: a Consolidated Meta-Database Platform for Biochemical Data Integration](https://arxiv.org/abs/2602.12310)
*Casper Asbjørn Eriksen,Jakob Lykke Andersen,Rolf Fagerberg,Daniel Merkle*

Main category: q-bio.QM

TL;DR: ChemRecon是一个用于整合和探索多个异构生物化学数据库的元数据库和Python接口，提供统一的查询、跨数据库分析和图表示构建功能。


<details>
  <summary>Details</summary>
Motivation: 生物化学数据分散在多个异构数据库中，缺乏统一的访问和分析方式，难以进行跨数据库的信息提取和冲突解决。

Method: 通过整合化合物、反应、酶、分子结构和原子映射等数据到一致的语义框架中，构建元数据库并提供Python接口，支持图遍历和跨数据库连接分析。

Result: 开发了ChemRecon系统，提供统一的查询接口，能够从冲突数据源中提取共识信息（如最可能的分子结构），支持跨数据库分析。

Conclusion: ChemRecon解决了生物化学数据库的异构性问题，为研究人员提供了强大的跨数据库分析和信息提取工具，促进了生物化学数据的整合利用。

Abstract: In this paper, we present ChemRecon, a meta-database and Python interface for integrating and exploring biochemical data across multiple heterogeneous resources by consolidating compounds, reactions, enzymes, molecular structures, and atom-to-atom maps from several major databases into a single, consistent ontology. ChemRecon enables unified querying, cross-database analysis, and the construction of graph-based representations of sets of related database entries by the traversal of inter-database connections. This facilitates information extraction which is impossible within any single database, including deriving consensus information from conflicting sources, of which identifying the most probable molecular structure associated with a given compound is just one example. The Python interface is available via pip from the Python Package Index (https://pypi.org/project/chemrecon/). ChemRecon is open-source and the source code is hosted at GitLab (https://gitlab.com/casbjorn/chemrecon). Documentation and additional information is available at https://chemrecon.org.

### [2] [Free Lunch in Medical Image Foundation Model Pre-training via Randomized Synthesis and Disentanglement](https://arxiv.org/abs/2602.12317)
*Yuhan Wei,Yuting He,Linshan Wu,Fuxiang Huang,Junlin Hou,Hao Chen*

Main category: q-bio.QM

TL;DR: RaSD框架使用随机合成数据预训练医学影像基础模型，通过建模解剖结构和外观变化，在合成数据上实现可扩展、隐私保护的基础模型训练。


<details>
  <summary>Details</summary>
Motivation: 医学影像基础模型发展受限于大规模标注数据的稀缺性、异质性和高成本，需要寻找替代方案来突破数据瓶颈。

Method: 提出RaSD框架，使用随机高斯分布建模解剖结构和外观变化，在合成数据上进行预训练，通过多尺度结构和外观扰动迫使模型学习不变的任务相关解剖特征。

Result: 在1.2百万3D体积和9.6百万2D图像上预训练，在6种成像模态、48个数据集、56个下游任务中评估，RaSD始终优于从头训练模型，在17个任务中表现最佳，在大多数任务中与真实数据预训练模型相当。

Conclusion: 合成数据本身具备驱动稳健表征学习的能力，为医学AI建立了范式转变，证明合成数据可以作为可扩展、隐私保护、临床可泛化基础模型的"免费午餐"。

Abstract: Medical image foundation models (MIFMs) have demonstrated remarkable potential for a wide range of clinical tasks, yet their development is constrained by the scarcity, heterogeneity, and high cost of large-scale annotated datasets. Here, we propose RaSD (Randomized Synthesis and Disentanglement), a scalable framework for pre-training MIFMs entirely on synthetic data. By modeling anatomical structures and appearance variations with randomized Gaussian distributions, RaSD exposes models to sufficient multi-scale structural and appearance perturbations, forcing them to rely on invariant and task-relevant anatomical cues rather than dataset-specific textures, thereby enabling robust and transferable representation learning. We pre-trained RaSD on 1.2 million 3D volumes and 9.6 million 2D images, and extensively evaluated the resulting models across 6 imaging modalities, 48 datasets, and 56 downstream tasks. Across all evaluated downstream tasks, RaSD consistently outperforms training-from-scratch models, achieves the best performance on 17 tasks, and remains comparable to models pre-trained on large real datasets in most others. These results demonstrate that the capacity of synthetic data alone to drive robust representation learning. Our findings establish a paradigm shift in medical AI, demonstrating that synthetic data can serve as a "free lunch" for scalable, privacy-preserving, and clinically generalizable foundation models.

### [3] [EMERALD-UI: An interactive web application to unveil novel protein biology hidden in the suboptimal-alignment space](https://arxiv.org/abs/2602.12730)
*Andrei Preoteasa,Andreas Grigorjew,Alexandru I. Tomescu,Hajk-Georg Drost*

Main category: q-bio.QM

TL;DR: EMERALD-UI是一个开源交互式网络应用，用于可视化次优比对空间中隐藏的稳定结构构象或蛋白质区域，揭示传统最优比对方法忽略的生物学信息。


<details>
  <summary>Details</summary>
Motivation: 蛋白质序列比对是探索蛋白质进化和功能适应的关键技术，但传统最优比对方法往往忽略次优比对配置中可能出现的蛋白质保守模式和功能变化差异。

Method: 开发EMERALD-UI开源交互式网络应用，通过可视化次优比对空间中的稳定结构构象或蛋白质区域，揭示传统方法忽略的生物学信息。

Result: EMERALD-UI已作为开源工具发布，可通过https://algbio.github.io/emerald-ui/访问，帮助研究人员探索次优比对空间中的隐藏生物学信息。

Conclusion: EMERALD-UI通过可视化次优比对空间，能够揭示传统最优比对方法忽略的蛋白质保守模式和功能变化，为探索未发现的生物学提供新工具。

Abstract: Life over the past four billion years has been shaped by proteins and their capacity to assemble into three dimensional conformations. Protein sequence alignments have been the enabling technology for exploring the evolution and functional adaptation of proteins across the tree of life. Recent advancements in scaling the prediction of three dimensional protein structures from primary sequence alone, revealed that different modes of conservation and function operate on the sequence and structure level. This difference in protein conservation patterns and their underlying functional change that could emerge in suboptimal alignment configurations is often ignored in optimal protein alignment approaches. We introduce EMERALD-UI, an open-source interactive web application which is designed to reveal unexplored biology by visualising stable structural conformations or protein regions hidden in the suboptimal alignment space.
  Availability: EMERALD-UI is available at https://algbio.github.io/emerald-ui/.
  Contact: hdrost001@dundee.ac.uk or alexandru.tomescu@helsinki.fi.

### [4] [LinkedNN: a neural model of linkage disequilibrium decay for recent effective population size inference](https://arxiv.org/abs/2602.13121)
*Chris C R Smith*

Main category: q-bio.PE

TL;DR: 开发了一个基于神经网络的生物信息学工具，通过自动计算连锁不平衡相关特征来估计近期有效种群大小，在稀疏、未分型数据上优于现有方法


<details>
  <summary>Details</summary>
Motivation: 分子生态学应用中常面临稀疏、未分型的基因组数据，现有方法在这种数据条件下表现不佳，需要一种能够利用少量测序个体和变异位点就能准确估计近期有效种群大小的方法

Method: 使用神经网络自动计算多态性位点间基因组距离相关的连锁不平衡特征，开发了名为LinkedNN的Python软件包

Result: 新方法在相对较少的测序个体和变异位点条件下，优于现有的深度学习和基于汇总统计的方法，特别适用于稀疏、未分型数据

Conclusion: LinkedNN为分子生态学应用提供了一个高效、准确的工具，能够处理稀疏基因组数据并准确估计近期有效种群大小

Abstract: Summary: A bioinformatics tool is presented for estimating recent effective population size by using a neural network to automatically compute linkage disequilibrium-related features as a function of genomic distance between polymorphisms. The new method outperforms existing deep learning and summary statistic-based approaches using relatively few sequenced individuals and variant sites, making it particularly valuable for molecular ecology applications with sparse, unphased data. Availability and implementation: The program is available as an easily installable Python package with documentation here: https://pypi.org/project/linkedNN/. The open source code is available from: https://github.com/the-smith-lab/LinkedNN.

</details>
