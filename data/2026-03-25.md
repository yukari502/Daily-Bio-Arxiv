### [1] [SynLeaF: A Dual-Stage Multimodal Fusion Framework for Synthetic Lethality Prediction Across Pan- and Single-Cancer Contexts](https://arxiv.org/abs/2603.22369)
*Zheming Xing,Siyuan Zhou,Ruinan Wang,Rui Han,Shiming Zhang,Shiqu Chen,Yurui Huang,Jiahao Ma,Yifan Chen,Xuan Wang,Yadong Wang,Junyi Li*

Main category: q-bio.GN

TL;DR: SynLeaF是一个用于预测合成致死性的双阶段多模态融合框架，通过知识蒸馏缓解模态惰性，在泛癌和单癌场景中均表现出色。


<details>
  <summary>Details</summary>
Motivation: 合成致死性预测对癌症药物开发很重要，但现有多模态方法存在"模态惰性"问题，导致不同模态收敛速度不一致，难以有效融合异构多源数据，限制了在泛癌和单癌预测中的表现。

Method: 提出SynLeaF双阶段多模态融合框架：1) 使用基于VAE的交叉编码器和专家乘积机制融合四种组学数据；2) 使用关系图卷积网络从生物医学知识图谱中捕获结构化基因表示；3) 引入双阶段训练机制，采用特征级知识蒸馏和自适应单模态教师与集成策略缓解模态惰性。

Result: 在8种特定癌症类型和泛癌数据集上的广泛实验中，SynLeaF在19个场景中的17个取得最优性能。消融研究和梯度分析验证了所提融合和蒸馏机制对模型鲁棒性和泛化能力的关键贡献。

Conclusion: SynLeaF通过创新的双阶段多模态融合和知识蒸馏机制，有效解决了模态惰性问题，在泛癌和单癌合成致死性预测中均表现出优越性能，为癌症药物开发提供了有力工具。

Abstract: Accurate prediction of synthetic lethality (SL) is important for guiding the development of cancer drugs and therapies. SL prediction faces significant challenges in the effective fusion of heterogeneous multi-source data. Existing multimodal methods often suffer from "modality laziness" due to disparate convergence speeds, which hinders the exploitation of complementary information. This is also one reason why most existing SL prediction models cannot perform well on both pan-cancer and single-cancer SL pair prediction. In this study, we propose SynLeaF, a dual-stage multimodal fusion framework for SL prediction across pan- and single-cancer contexts. The framework employs a VAE-based cross-encoder with a product of experts mechanism to fuse four omics data types (gene expression, mutation, methylation, and CNV), while simultaneously utilizing a relational graph convolutional network to capture structured gene representations from biomedical knowledge graphs. To mitigate modality laziness, SynLeaF introduces a dual-stage training mechanism employing featurelevel knowledge distillation with adaptive uni-modal teacher and ensemble strategies. In extensive experiments across eight specific cancer types and a pancancer dataset, SynLeaF achieves superior performance in 17 out of 19 scenarios. Ablation studies and gradient analyses further validate the critical contributions of the proposed fusion and distillation mechanisms to model robustness and generalization. To facilitate community use, a web server is available at https://synleaf.bioinformatics-lilab.cn.

### [2] [Subspace Tensor Orthogonal Rotation Model (STORM) for Batch Alignment, Cell Type Deconvolution, and Gene Imputation in Spatial Transcriptomic Data](https://arxiv.org/abs/2603.22477)
*Sean Cottrell,Guo-Wei Wei,Longxiu Huang*

Main category: q-bio.QM

TL;DR: STORM提出了一种新颖的子空间张量正交旋转模型，通过不规则张量分解将多个空间转录组切片整合到共享潜在空间，解决批次效应、细胞类型混合信号和基因表达缺失问题。


<details>
  <summary>Details</summary>
Motivation: 空间转录组数据分析面临三大挑战：批次效应处理、混合细胞类型信号处理、以及测量不佳或缺失基因表达的插补。现有方法在这些问题上仍有局限。

Method: 提出STORM模型，采用不规则张量分解技术，将多个基因表达矩阵分解并整合到共享潜在空间，考虑物理模式或微环境层面，实现多切片对齐。

Result: 数值实验表明，STORM在垂直和水平批次整合、细胞类型去卷积以及未测量基因插补方面达到了最先进的性能。

Conclusion: STORM为空间转录组数据分析提供了一种可解释的解决方案，相比黑盒深度学习方法具有内在可解释性优势。

Abstract: Spatial transcriptomics data analysis integrates cellular transcriptional activity with spatial coordinates to identify spatial domains, infer cell-type dynamics, and characterize gene expression patterns within tissues. Despite recent advances, significant challenges remain, including the treatment of batch effects, the handling of mixed cell-type signals, and the imputation of poorly measured or missing gene expression. This work addresses these challenges by introducing a novel Subspace Tensor Orthogonal Rotation Model (STORM) that aligns multiple slices which vary in their spatial dimensions and geometry by considering them at the level of physical patterns or microenvironments. To this end, STORM presents an irregular tensor factorization technique for decomposing a collection of gene expression matrices and integrating them into a shared latent space for downstream analysis. In contrast to black-box deep learning approaches, the proposed model is inherently interpretable. Numerical experiments demonstrate state-of-the-art performance in vertical and horizontal batch integration, cell-type deconvolution, and unmeasured gene imputation for spatial transcriptomics data.

### [3] [Central Dogma Transformer III: Interpretable AI Across DNA, RNA, and Protein](https://arxiv.org/abs/2603.23361)
*Nobuyuki Ota*

Main category: cs.LG

TL;DR: CDT-III是一个机制导向的AI模型，通过虚拟细胞嵌入器架构模拟细胞空间区室化，在DNA、RNA和蛋白质三个层面预测细胞响应，实现了RNA预测r=0.843和蛋白质预测r=0.969的高精度。


<details>
  <summary>Details</summary>
Motivation: 当前生物AI模型虽然能预测复杂的细胞响应，但其学习到的表征与它们试图捕捉的分子过程脱节。需要开发能够连接分子机制与预测性能的机制导向AI模型。

Method: 提出CDT-III模型，采用两阶段虚拟细胞嵌入器架构：VCE-N模拟细胞核中的转录过程，VCE-C模拟细胞质中的翻译过程。模型覆盖中心法则全过程（DNA、RNA、蛋白质），通过下游任务正则化上游表征。

Result: 在5个保留基因上，RNA预测r=0.843，蛋白质预测r=0.969。蛋白质监督提高了DNA层面的可解释性（CTCF富集增加30%）。在CD52敲除模拟中，正确预测29/29蛋白质变化，无需临床数据重新发现5/7已知临床副作用。

Conclusion: CDT-III展示了机制导向AI在连接分子过程与预测性能方面的潜力，蛋白质监督能正则化上游表征并提高可解释性。梯度分析仅需未扰动基线数据即可筛选所有基因，为药物副作用预测提供了高效工具。

Abstract: Biological AI models increasingly predict complex cellular responses, yet their learned representations remain disconnected from the molecular processes they aim to capture. We present CDT-III, which extends mechanism-oriented AI across the full central dogma: DNA, RNA, and protein. Its two-stage Virtual Cell Embedder architecture mirrors the spatial compartmentalization of the cell: VCE-N models transcription in the nucleus and VCE-C models translation in the cytosol. On five held-out genes, CDT-III achieves per-gene RNA r=0.843 and protein r=0.969. Adding protein prediction improves RNA performance (r=0.804 to 0.843), demonstrating that downstream tasks regularize upstream representations. Protein supervision sharpens DNA-level interpretability, increasing CTCF enrichment by 30%. Applied to in silico CD52 knockdown approximating Alemtuzumab, the model predicts 29/29 protein changes correctly and rediscovers 5 of 7 known clinical side effects without clinical data. Gradient-based side effect profiling requires only unperturbed baseline data (r=0.939), enabling screening of all 2,361 genes without new experiments.
