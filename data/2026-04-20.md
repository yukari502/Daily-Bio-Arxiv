### [1] [Dual-Timescale Memory in a Spiking Neuron-Astrocyte Network for Efficient Navigation](https://arxiv.org/abs/2604.15391)
*Yuliya Tsybina,Evgenia Antonova,Sergey Shchanikov,Vsevolod Kulagin,Alexey Mikhaylov,Victor Kazantsev,Vyacheslav Demin,Susanna Gordleeva*

Main category: q-bio.QM

TL;DR: 提出受神经-星形胶质细胞动力学启发的双时间尺度记忆机制，通过脉冲神经元-星形胶质细胞网络(SNAN)结合长时程记忆和短时抑制，在部分可观测环境中显著提升导航性能。

  <summary>Details</summary>
Motivation: 生物智能能在复杂环境中结合长时记忆和短时位置抑制进行导航，而人工系统在部分可观测条件下难以实现类似能力。受神经和星形胶质细胞互补时间尺度动力学启发，需要开发新的记忆机制来改善探索-利用权衡。

Method: 设计脉冲神经元-星形胶质细胞网络(SNAN)：STDP在远时间尺度强化成功动作序列，星形胶质细胞钙瞬变在短时间尺度抑制最近访问状态，形成双时间尺度记忆机制。将STDP映射到忆阻器VTEAM模型，在交叉阵列上实现硬件验证。

Result: 在极端部分可观测的网格世界导航任务中，SNAN将中位路径长度减少高达6倍，大幅提高目标完成率。硬件实现相比CPU获得数量级的速度/面积和能量/决策优势。星形胶质细胞调节自然缓解探索-利用权衡。

Conclusion: 星形胶质细胞启发的双时间尺度记忆机制是可扩展、硬件可实现的原理，为神经形态机器人和边缘AI系统提供新方案。局部感觉数据调节可视为新型工作记忆"拓扑上下文记忆"。

Abstract: Biological agents navigate complex environments by combining long-term memory of successful actions with short-term suppression of recently visited locations-a capability that remains difficult to replicate in artificial systems, especially under partial observability. Inspired by the complementary timescales of neural and astrocytic dynamics, we introduce a spiking neuron-astrocyte network (SNAN) where spike-timing-dependent plasticity (STDP) reinforces successful action sequences on a distant time scale, while astrocytic calcium transients suppress recently visited states on a short-term time scale, effectively blocking locations already explored. This dual-timescale memory mechanism biases the agent toward unexplored regions, accelerating goal finding without requiring explicit global statistics. We show that in grid-world navigation tasks with extreme partial observability, SNAN reduces median path length by up to sixfold and drastically improves goal completion rates compared to baseline agents. The astrocytic modulation inherently mitigates the exploration-exploitation trade-off as an emergent consequence of local state suppression. This kind of local sensory data modulation can be considered as a new type of working memory referred to as a "Topological-Context Memory". To validate hardware feasibility using neuromorphic approaches, we map STDP to a memristive VTEAM model and implement a subset of the network on a crossbar array, achieving order-of-magnitude gains in speed per area and energy per decision over CPU implementations. Our results establish astrocyte-inspired dual-timescale memory as a scalable, hardware-realizable principle for neuromorphic robotics and edge-AI systems.
