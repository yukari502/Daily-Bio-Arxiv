### [1] [Thoughtseeds as Latent Causes: A Dual-Process Computational Phenomenology of Focused-Attention Meditation](https://arxiv.org/abs/2607.14833)
*Prakash Chandra Kavi,Daniel Ari Friedman,Gustavo Patow*

Main category: q-bio.NC

TL;DR: The paper presents a computational model of focused-attention meditation using active inference and a three-layer neural architecture (physiological substrate, generative model, metacognitive monitor) that simulates expert vs. novice meditation dynamics, reproducing empirical findings in contemplative neuroscience.


<details>
  <summary>Details</summary>
Motivation: To bridge first-person phenomenology of meditation (focused attention, mind-wandering, meta-awareness) with objective neurophysiological measures through a formal computational framework.

Method: A dual-process active inference model with a three-layer nested Markov-blanket architecture: L1 (Ornstein-Uhlenbeck process over Yeo networks), L2 (System 1 generative model with thoughtseeds), L3 (System 2 metacognitive Global Neuronal Workspace). Uses variational Expectation-Maximization for training expert vs. novice phenotypes.

Result: Simulations reproduce empirical patterns in contemplative neuroscience, showing distinct attractor states (breath focus, mind-wandering, meta-awareness, redirect attention) and differences between expert and novice meditation phenotypes.

Conclusion: The model provides a tractable computational link between subjective meditative experience and objective neural dynamics, offering a formal account of how meta-awareness (as GNW ignition) enables attentional regulation during meditation.

Abstract: Meditative expertise involves sustained attention, rapid recovery from distraction, and coordinated dynamics of large-scale brain networks. We present a computational phenomenology of focused-attention meditation traversing four attractor states: breath focus, mind-wandering, meta-awareness, and redirect attention. Within a dual-process active inference formulation, the model implements a three-layer nested Markov-blanket architecture: (L1) a high-dimensional physiological neuronal substrate modeled as a stochastic multivariate Ornstein--Uhlenbeck process over attentional Yeo networks; (L2) a low-dimensional generative model (System 1) that encodes latent mental content as thoughtseeds and evaluates autonomic action tendencies; and (L3) an agentic metacognitive monitor (System 2) that implements a Global Neuronal Workspace (GNW) capacity bottleneck to selectively gate these tendencies. In L3, meta-awareness functions as the GNW ignition signal, derived from policy-prior divergence and dynamically gated by direct competition between orchestrator and distractor thoughtseeds. Policy selection actively minimizes expected free energy, and L2 actions furnish descending predictions over network activity to close the enactive perception--action cycle. Training uses variational Expectation-Maximization (EM) across expert and novice phenotypes. Simulations reproduce behavior consistent with empirical observations and findings in contemplative neuroscience, providing a tractable link between first-person phenomenology and objective neurophysiological measures.

### [2] [Infectious Disease Induces Emergent Oscillations, Extinction and Changes in Community Persistence in a Food Chain](https://arxiv.org/abs/2607.14360)
*Hooman Saveh,Fakhteh Ghanbarnejad*

Main category: q-bio.PE

TL;DR: This paper studies a food chain model (prey → predators → apex predators) using Lotka-Volterra equations combined with an SIR model applied to one species at a time, introducing a parameter that modifies predation/hunting rates for infected species. Key findings: infected predators show oscillations but never go extinct; infected apex predators may go extinct under specific parameters but show no oscillations; community persistence increases for predators but decreases for apex predators.


<details>
  <summary>Details</summary>
Motivation: Most food web models do not account for infectious diseases. This study aims to bridge ecology and epidemiology by incorporating disease dynamics into a simple food chain to understand how infections at different trophic levels affect population stability and survival.

Method: The authors model a three-species food chain (prey, predators, apex predators) using generalized Lotka-Volterra equations, with an SIR model applied to one species at a time. They introduce a parameter w that increases predation rate by factor w and decreases hunting rate by factor 1/w for infected individuals.

Result: Disease in predators: no extinction occurs, but oscillations appear under certain parameters (not seen in standard SIR or Lotka-Volterra alone). Disease in apex predators: no oscillations, but extinction may occur in specific parameter ranges. Community persistence increases for predators and decreases for apex predators.

Conclusion: Infectious diseases affect trophic levels differently in a food chain: predators are more resilient (never go extinct, show oscillations), while apex predators face extinction risk. This highlights the importance of considering disease in ecological models for predicting community stability.

Abstract: Food webs have been extensively studied from both ecological and mathematical aspects. However, most of the models studied in this area do not capture the effects of infectious diseases simultaneously. Recently, the idea of including an infectious disease in a food web model has been investigated. We study and simulate a small food chain consisting of only prey, predators, and apex predators governed by the generalized Lotka-Volterra equations, and we implement the Susceptible-Infected-Recovered (SIR) model on only one of the species at a time in the food chain. To study the effects of an infectious disease on the food chain, we introduce a new parameter that increases the predation rate by a factor of $w$ and decreases the hunting rate by a factor of $1/w$ for infected species. When the infectious disease is present in predators, we observe that predators do not become extinct under any set of parameters; however, an oscillation in their population size occurs under some circumstances, which we do not observe in ordinary SIR or the generalized Lotka-Volterra equations alone. When an infectious disease is present in apex predators, oscillations in the population size do not happen; but if the set of parameters is in a specific range the apex predators may become extinct. Furthermore, the chance of survival of the community, known as community persistence, increases for the predators and decreases for the apex predators.

Method: The authors model random LGT events as a Poisson process with a constant transfer rate. They derive gene tree and site pattern probabilities for the simplest cases of two and three species.

Abstract: Processes such as incomplete lineage sorting (ILS) and reticulate evolution (arising, for example, from hybridization and lateral gene transfer (LGT)) are known to cause discordance between gene trees and species trees, complicating phylogenetic inference. While the multispecies coalescent model has led to a rich theoretical understanding of ILS, probabilistic models for LGT have received comparatively less attention. Here, we revisit a basic LGT model in which random LGT events occur according to a Poisson process with a constant transfer rate. Focusing on the simplest cases of two and three species, we derive gene tree and site pattern probabilities and discuss their implications for model identifiability. We also address the question of whether LGT and ILS can be distinguished from one another under these probabilistic models. We discuss empirical applications and outline directions for future work.

### [6] [A Validated Data-driven Subject and Vehicle Specific Nonlinear Biodynamic Model for Predicting Upper-Body Response in Vehicle Ride](https://arxiv.org/abs/2607.14457)

  <summary>Details</summary>
Motivation: To create a subject-specific biodynamic model that can predict human response under unknown loading conditions, useful for occupational health assessment and protective equipment design.

Method: A 6-DOF lumped-parameter model with nonlinear viscoelastic joints is formulated. Fifteen subject-specific parameters are identified using a hybrid two-phase optimization: PSO for global search on a frequency-domain surrogate, followed by Nelder-Mead refinement on the full nonlinear ODE. Multi-case optima are fused via error-weighted geometric mean in log space.

Result: The idealized consensus parameter set retains physical interpretability across loading scenarios and successfully predicts the subject's experimental response in forward dynamics.

Conclusion: The framework provides a complete pipeline from experimental data through optimization, idealization, and forward prediction, suitable for diverse loading scenarios in occupational health and protective equipment design.

Abstract: A six-degree-of-freedom (6-DOF) nonlinear lumped-parameter biodynamic model of the seated human upper body is formulated to predict human response under different unknown loading conditions. The model consists of six anatomically partitioned cascade body segments: pelvis, lower torso, central torso, upper torso, neck, and head. The joints are connected through nonlinear viscoelastic joints incorporating strain-stiffening restoring forces consistent with the nonlinear constitutive behavior of biological soft tissue. Fifteen subject-specific mechanical parameters are considered as design variables. The design variables are six joint natural frequencies, six viscous damping ratios, one Rayleigh coefficient, one nonlinear stiffening scale, and one mass scale. The subject-specific design variables are identified from three distinct vehicular loading cases using a hybrid two-phase optimization strategy. Particle Swarm Optimization (PSO) globally searches on a frequency-domain surrogate and seeds a bounded Nelder-Mead refinement for the full nonlinear Ordinary Differential Equation (ODE) response. A rigorous parameter idealization methodology, based on error-weighted geometric mean in log space, was implemented to fuse the multi-case optima into a single subject-representative consensus parameter set. The idealized model retains physical interpretability across loading scenarios and is subsequently used in forward dynamics to predict and validate the subject's experimental response. The framework constitutes a complete subject-specific biodynamic pipeline, from experimental data through optimization, idealization, and forward prediction, suitable for occupational health assessment and protective equipment design across diverse loading scenarios.

  <summary>Details</summary>

It matches the strongest token-based model in multi-study integration while preserving more biological structure, without batch labels. Abstract: Most single-cell foundation models are adapted from language models, representing each cell as a sequence of gene tokens. This discards the relationships among genes and often the magnitude of their expression. We present scVision, a vision foundation model that instead renders each cell as a continuous image. By preserving expression magnitude and gene relationships, scVision reframes single-cell representation learning as a vision problem, connecting it to the mature methods of computer vision.
